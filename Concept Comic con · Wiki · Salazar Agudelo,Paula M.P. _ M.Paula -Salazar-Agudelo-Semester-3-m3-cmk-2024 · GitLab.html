    Introduction

    Project Goals Pixel Paranoia’s aim is to redefine event engagement for younger audiences, specifically through interactive and digital elements that make experiences more memorable and fun. Collaborating with ROSH Studios, the project’s focus is on creating adaptable solutions that enhance convention experiences like Comic Con, aligning with Gen Z’s expectations.

    Client Project

    Project Background

    Pixel Paranoia was approached by ROSH Studios to design a dynamic event activation aimed at Gen Z attendees. With a focus on creativity and adaptability, our goal is to build a fun, interactive experience that resonates with young audiences.

    Plan of Action

    To manage this project effectively, the team adopted a structured workflow:

    1. Task Management:
      • Used Trello to organize tasks into Discover, Define, Develop, and Deliver sprints.
      • Tracked progress with labeled milestones, daily Morning meetings, and feedback integration.
    2. Documentation:
      • Centralized all files and updates using Microsoft Teams, ensuring real-time collaboration and file access.
    3. Team Collaboration:
      • Assigned roles are based on individual strengths, and I am the Notes Picker of the group; I take notes for each meeting when we meet with our client.
      • I also conducted regular meeting review sessions to align deliverables with project goals.

    Frameworks and Methodologies

    DOT Framework

    The DOT Framework was important in organizing and doing the ConQuest project, making clear phases. Each part—Design and Technology—played a role in making our scavenger hunt idea for Comic-Con Utrecht successful.

    1. Design:
      • I participated in co-creation sessions where the team and stakeholders, like ROSH Studios, collaborated to give feedback on the progress of the scavenger hunt PWA concept.
      • I work on Figma prototypes, translating user insights into clear and interactive design solutions.
      • Focused on creating gamified elements, such as badges and QR code tasks, aligned with Gen Z’s preferences for engaging and fun experiences.
    2. Technology:
      • Led the creation of the PWA using scalable and easy-to-use technologies, adding features such as QR code scanning for tracking progress and interactive maps for smooth navigation.
      • Supported user testing and iterations, enabling technical adjustments like improving map clarity and refining task flows based on live feedback at Comic-Con Utrecht and other iterations we do after.

    MoSCoW Prioritization

    The MoSCoW framework was applied to prioritize features for the ConQuest PWA, ensuring that development focused on delivering essential functionality while leaving room for enhancements in future iterations.

    Prioritized Features

    1. Must-Have:
      These features formed the core functionality of the app:
      • Hunting and Finding Items: Players searched for hidden objects or items in themed rooms.
      • Visual Progression Tracking: Provided players with a clear sense of their progress.
      • Room Themes and Badges: Each room had distinct themes and badges to create structure and a sense of achievement.
      • Registration Posters: Clear starting points for players to join the game.
      • Rewards System: Motivated players to complete tasks with engaging rewards.
      • QR Codes: Enabled Map areas or tasks.
      • Interactive Event Map: Guided players through the event, connecting people, booths, and places.
    2. Should-Have:
      Features that enhanced engagement but weren’t essential for functionality:
      • Group Game Tasks: Promoted collaboration among players.
      • Tablet Integration: Added depth with interactive puzzles and Questions.
      • Progress Tracking: Allowed players to see their journey.
      • Dynamic Map Updates: Map adjusted in real-time based on player actions.
    3. Could-Have:
      Features to consider if additional time or resources were available:
      • Mini-Games or Side Quests: Offered bonus rewards for optional tasks.
      • Special Character (TIFFO): Served as a guide or mascot for the game.
      • Enhanced Map Features: Included animations or sound effects.
      • Different Reward Types: Personalized incentives for players.
    4. Won’t-Have:
      Features reserved for future iterations:
      • Complex AR Integration: Not essential for this version.
      • Physical Prizes: Focused on digital rewards for cost efficiency.
      • Solo Game Rooms: Kept the game group-focused for this iteration.

    Outcome

    • Focused Development: Delivered all Must-Have features to ensure a functional and engaging base.
    • Enhanced Engagement: Planned for future iterations with scalable ideas like mini-games and AR elements.
    • Budget-Friendly Execution: Excluded non-essential features (e.g., physical prizes, solo play) to stay within scope.

    Discover Phase

    Target audience

    Demographic

    • We're making a product for 16–27-year-olds – the convention attracts 11–27-year-olds.
    • The main audience is from the Netherlands but also diverse, so we'll make the concept in English.
    • Everyone is in a group.
    • People use TikTok, Instagram, Discord, and Reddit.
    • People stay for the whole convention.

    Based on our survey, observations at Comic-Con, and triangulation with personas and user journeys, we were able to successfully validate our target audience.

    Likes

    • Group activities are heavily preferred – social interaction.

    Game Types

    • 70% like interactive workshops/booths.
    • 53% like (gaming) competitions.
    • 41% like scavenger hunts & live/audience participation.
    • 29% like trading cards.
    • 18% like mobile games & VR/AR.
    • People like trivia and quizzes.

    Subjects

    • Heavy interest in cosplay.
    • People like celebrities.
    • People like anime.
    • People like creative things (e.g., drawing, art, etc.).

    Game Outcomes

    • People like merchandise as rewards.
    • People like memorabilia as rewards.
    • People want to show off their interests and skills.

    Dislikes

    • High prices – bad!
    • Long waiting times – bad!
    • Food and very niche media like board games are not popular.
    • Apps are not desired.
    • VR is not desired.

    Objective: To understand what Gen Z likes, needs, and how they engage with events like Comic Con.

    Key Activities

    Client Alignment

    • We held an initial call with ROSH Studios to discuss goals and project objectives. They emphasized creating a fun, adaptable experience that would appeal to Gen Z, giving us clear direction.

      Observations at Comic-Con

    I conducted on-site observations, capturing photos of popular booths and activities to understand what engaged attendees. This helped us identify interactive and gamified elements that resonate with Gen Z.

    • High Engagement Activities: Interactive booths like “Ghost Corps,” “Padawan Outpost,” and events like cosplay contests and Just Dance zones were highly engaging, attracting large groups of participants. Cosplay activities, in particular, fostered a sense of community and creativity​​.
    • Less Interactive Booths: Static booths, like those selling generic merchandise or food, had significantly lower engagement​​.
    • Social and Inclusive Dynamics: Cosplay and group activities were key engagement drivers. Observations highlighted that attendees often interacted socially, with many taking photos, making TikToks, or enjoying the welcoming environment​​.

    Survey Distribution

    • Although I didn’t create the survey questions, I shared them with Comic-Con attendees to gather direct feedback, adding valuable insights into Gen Z preferences.

      "Surveys distributed during Comic-Con showed strong interest in gamified and social experiences, with 53.3% of respondents favoring interactive activities. Suggestions included scavenger hunts and workshops, emphasizing the importance of fostering connection and creativity."

      Library Research on Geek and Nerd Culture

    • I conducted research on geek and nerd culture to understand what keeps these communities engaged, guiding us to define our concept.

      Library Research on Digitalization

    • I explored how digitalization (VR, AR) enhances event experiences because, initially, this was the idea with Gen Z’s tech engagement.

    • Explored nerd/geek culture and digitalization’s role in enhancing event experiences.

    • In the beginning, the idea was to include tools like AR or VR for a tool to engage our users, but with our surveys and interviews, we decided not to include it.

    All these areas you can check for more understanding

    • Literature Research: Digitalization and Culture
    • Flowchart Diagram: I made a diagram that helps us know how to start the flow of each question in the app. That allows us to make a starting point for our prototype.

    Interviews

    • I conducted three interviews with attendees at Comic-Con, while other team members conducted five additional interviews. These provided insights into Gen Z’s engagement preferences.

      Results of our interview

      • Key Motivations:
        • Cosplay Enthusiasm: Cosplay culture is a major draw, both for participants and spectators.
        • Merchandise: Anime posters, figurines, and cosplay accessories are highly sought after.
        • Social Atmosphere: Attendees value the inclusive and creative environment in which they can connect with others.
      • Strengths of the Convention:
        • Variety: Wide range of merchandise and displays, including Art Alley and themed areas like Doctor Who.
        • Cosplay and Visual Appeal: Creative displays and cosplay competitions enhance engagement.
      • Areas for Improvement:
        • Interactive Activities: Attendees expressed interest in:
          • Gaming tournaments.
          • Drawing contests.
          • Cosplay-focused workshops.
        • Accessibility and Comfort: Complaints included high food prices and a lack of seating.
      • Opportunities:
        • Increase engagement with more gamified, team-based challenges and cosplay-specific photo booths or workshops.
        • Address comfort concerns to improve overall satisfaction.

    Discover Validation Process

    • Initial Review: I ask for feedback about the competitive analysis I presented to teacher Jan on Monday. In all group meetings, I shared my updates, and my group managed to gather information that improved our understanding of the project.
    • Client Check-in: We shared findings with ROSH Studios to get feedback and improve our strategy. The client agreed with what we found and mentioned that our idea should rely on the validation of our research.

    Define Phase

    Goal: Improve project aims and outline a clear idea based on findings from the Discover Phase.

    Key Activities

    1. Organizing Insights:

      • Used Lindy’s affinity diagram to group insights into key themes, such as engagement, task clarity, and social interaction.
      • Insights from surveys and observations guided the focus on gamified and interactive solutions.

    2. Competitive and SWOT Analysis:

      • Conducted a SWOT analysis, highlighting strengths in gamification and opportunities for scaling through innovative engagement methods by the observation at the first comic-con we go.
      • The competitive analysis identified gaps in comic-con events, such as limited interactivity of digital things, validating the need for more digitalization in the comic-con systems in Amsterdam.

      For example, Marvel at NYCC offers live events and exclusive giveaways like the Marvel Move app but uses AR/VR moderately, limiting its broader digital interactivity. In comparison, Comic-Con Amsterdam focuses heavily on live experiences like cosplay contests and merchandise displays, as observed during the **Cosplay Contest setup,** where large, engaged crowds are placed but in so many different areas. When I made this competitive, as a group, we talked about the idea of using the tool of AR or VR. In the end was ,not what our users wanted, based on the survey .

    Competitive Comparison

    Feature Scavenger Hunt PWA Marvel NYCC Amazon Prime SDCC Comic-Con Amsterdam
    Gamification High – Interactive tasks High – Minimal tasks High – VR challenges Low – Physical booths
    AR/VR Integration Moderate – Scavenger focus Moderate – Limited High – Immersive VR None
    Social Media Engagement Strong – Badge sharing Strong – Hashtags Strong – Campaigns Limited only in TikTok
    User Interaction High – Task-driven flow Moderate – Photo ops High – Immersive tasks Low – Static booths

    3. Developing Personas:

    • Created three personas representing typical Gen Z attendees based on survey and interview data.
    • Focused on their motivations, Expectations, and preferences for interactive experiences.

    This process made three detailed personas, one being Mia Thompson, a 19-year-old Gen Z person who cares about creativity and community. The personas were made from survey and interview data to find out what typical attendees want, what bothers them, and what they like. Mia is the idealist type who is focused on creative expression, social interaction, and exploring at conventions. The personas helped make clear goals to improve engagement with interactive and creative activities while tackling common attendee issues, like needing better-organized areas, low-cost activities, and more hands-on experiences. These findings laid the groundwork for creating event experiences suitable for Gen Z.

    Define Conclusion

    In the Define phase, the main goal was to set clear project targets and improve the main idea. Using insights from the Discover phase, I worked with the team to develop a project plan based on research done at Comic-Con Amsterdam. We gathered survey results, conducted interviews, and made observations to create a persona for our target audience—Gen Z attendees. This persona showed important interests such as gamification and fun event experiences while noting issues like confusing navigation and unclear task descriptions. The Define phase included competitive and SWOT analyses to find strengths and weaknesses in current event comic-con. This work guided us to create a user-focused solution with gamified elements to boost engagement, but we didn't consider that we were at the convention and didn't have a booth or stand to show our concept. By outlining a clear user journey and highlighting essential features, the Define phase laid the groundwork for a design that appealed to both users and stakeholders.

    Define Validation Process

    1. Team Feedback Meetings:

      • The team came together to discuss our findings and outline our ideas. In this time, I developed the persona and helped on the user journey. We collaborated to complete our concept.

      Nevertheless, our presentation of the data led to missing some details. I think this occurred because we were constrained by a 30-minute time frame, which hindered our ability to explore all aspects and thoroughly explain our findings.

    2. Client Validation:

      • The client mentioned that the research results did not match due to different audiences in the two surveys. Therefore, we should concentrate on the relevant data. They were pleased with our idea but recommended that we should enhance the presentation with more visuals and offer a clear conclusion supported by strong research.

    Develop Phase

    Objective: Prototype and refine the app to meet user needs and expectations.

    During the development phase, I mostly worked on making and improving the Scavenger Hunt PWA according to what we brainstormed on making a started page and making the mag navigation based on the comic-con at Utrecht map we see online. This included making some sketches based on the map of the image and interactive models you see below, adding key features, doing repeated tests, and making updates based on what we found to increase usability and interest. I participated in each area and some iterations; you can check the page of the idea prototype.

    Key Activities

    Prototyping:

    Designed interactive Figma prototypes to visualize user navigation and task flow and make it interactive and clickable to the next page or section in Figma. You can check more information on the Figma prototype concept idea page for more explanation.

    PWA Development:

    The PWA Development Phase aimed at making a scavenger hunt app for Comic-Con visitors. Important features were pop-up feedback for tasks and map blips. I coded it to make the impression of seeing live location tracking. It was designed to work on mobile devices and even offline to ensure ease of the page of transferable code, which is the app concept explanation of what I do.

    User testing at Comic-Con Utrecht found problems like unclear instructions and hard navigation. This led to changes such as tutorials for new users and simpler task descriptions. Despite tight timelines and merge issues, the app was improved based on user input and event requirements. Plans include adding NFC and enhancing rewards to increase user engagement and scalability. That is one of our client's feedback improvements.

    User Testing:

    User testing showed me that being simple and clear is important at crowded events like Comic-Con. Many participants did not go through the onboarding tutorial, which led to confusion about navigation and tasks. This made it clear that we need a more engaging and required introduction. The blinking map markers frustrated users, so I switched them to static markers to clarify things. Task descriptions became simpler, and I added happy pop-ups for instant feedback, significantly boosting user engagement.

    A major lesson learned was that good promotion is very important; many attendees did not see the banners and QR codes, so they missed the scavenger hunt. We decided to use budget-friendly rewards, like Pepernoot, but also considered other incentives like discounts or merchandise to motivate participants. In summary, this experience highlighted the need to match app features with real-life event situations to ensure usability and strong engagement, even in busy and stressful environments. You can read more on the document or the page of user testing

    Develop Validation Process

    1. Concept Testing: I was involved in early prototypes and performed tests to check user engagement and how well they worked. Early tests showed problems like hard-to-follow navigation, vague task directions, and a lack of clear progress updates. From the feedback, I made important changes, like switching blinking map markers for steady ones, making task descriptions easier, and including celebratory pop-ups for quick feedback after tasks were done. Real-time testing at Comic-Con Utrecht and in controlled settings further improved these features, confirming they functioned effectively in real-life situations.
    2. Peer Reviews:
      • The feedback from peer reviews and client meetings showed that the idea was good, but the execution of the banners and promotion of our concept at the event could have been better. The app could boost engagement, but a few improvements were recommended, such as improved incentives, marketing, and interaction methods. This feedback guided our final adjustments, ensuring the app meets user needs and client expectations, which helps prepare for iterations and updates to our advice report. In the advice report page, you can see the iteration and implementation.

    Deliver Phase

    Objective: Finalize and present a functional, user-centered app that meets client expectations.

    Key Activities

    Polished PWA and Presentation to ROSH Studio:

    What We Did: We created a smartphone-compatible scavenger hunt web app that includes tasks with QR codes, badge-based rewards, navigation tools, and group activities.
    Result: Allowed 125 users to use QR codes even with many people around. However, no one finished all the tasks because of difficulties, such as too many people and poor visibility of materials.
    Feedback: The client saw potential in the idea but noted that improvements are necessary for visibility, marketing, and rewards.

    Replace QR codes with NFC tags or other technologies for more seamless and engaging user interactions.

    Conclusion:

    Post-Delivery Refinements:

    I help ask our teachers for feedback to improve the advice report for client feedback. I also include usability testing in the methodology to make it more convenient. The reference link shows the results of our research and testing. You can check the user testing finding document on the advice report page.
